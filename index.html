An entire movement was formed by new technology like cloud infrastructure and virtual machines. This cultural change in perspective combines the two teams into one lean mean rapid deployment machine, leveraging code to manage the infrastructure. It is called DevOps.

Transparency, collaboration and cross-functional teams with polyglot skills are breaking down the walls with automation and rapid deployment. Software gets shipped quickly, more often, code failures are detected and corrected faster and the product runs smoother. This sped up process allows innovation to flourish and companies to do more in less time.

DevOps - non-tech perspective

I have never thought myself working in tech industry. Or creating this website from a template with my own hands during couple of hours, having just primary HTML and CSS skills. But here I am, writing content to attract your attention.

It's hard not to see the evolution of technologies in our lives, in every aspect. So I have decided to have a seat closer to the main scene and take a tiny part in this process.

I'm addicted to learning and impoving things. And I admire people who can teach others, show ways, solve issues and meanwhile still learn, learn continuously. Maybe, that is why I like DevOps concept. It's not a TOOL, it's PEOPLE - peole, impoving their systems and desiring to improve them all the time. That is so awesome!

DevOps is a kind of philosophy. How can you sell philosophy? That's one more reason why I like it - for your own growth you share it with other people. Most companies say they want to change something. And most of them first need to change their mindset - start seeing hidden gems, alternative, more optimal ways, step away from formal thinking - it's not working so good any more.
